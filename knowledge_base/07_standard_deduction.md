# Standard Deduction for Salaried Individuals

## Overview
Standard deduction is a flat deduction from salary income — no receipts, no investment proof needed.
It replaced the earlier transport allowance (₹19,200) and medical reimbursement (₹15,000).

---

## Amounts by Year and Regime

| Financial Year | Old Regime | New Regime |
|---------------|-----------|-----------|
| FY 2023-24 | ₹50,000 | ₹50,000 |
| FY 2024-25 | ₹50,000 | ₹75,000 |
| FY 2025-26 | ₹75,000 | ₹75,000 |

### Key Change History:
- Budget 2024 (July): Increased new regime standard deduction from ₹50K to ₹75K for FY 2024-25
- Budget 2025 (Feb): Increased old regime standard deduction from ₹50K to ₹75K for FY 2025-26
- From FY 2025-26 onwards: ₹75,000 in BOTH regimes

## Who Can Claim
- Salaried employees (income under head "Salaries")
- Pensioners (pension is treated as salary)
- NOT available for: self-employed, freelancers, business income

## Professional Tax
- Separate from standard deduction
- State-level tax, typically ₹2,400/year (₹200/month) in most states
- Deductible from salary income under Section 16(iii) in BOTH regimes
- Maharashtra: max ₹2,500/year; Karnataka: max ₹2,400/year
- Usually already deducted by employer and shown in Form 16
